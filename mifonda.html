Mifonda Storefront

Apr 2024

Role: Designer | Focus: Figma Prototyping, Client Review, UI/UX

The challenge: MiFonda is a technology startup focused on helping small restaurants grow by helping them manage their online presence, providing ordering, pickup, and delivery service. They didn’t want to imitate the typical online ordering experience -- instead, they tasked my team to create a visual-foward, interactive storefront for both mobile and desktop users.

The solution: A video-centric platform with a creative yet organized ordering system.


1. Initial Brainstorm and Design

After our initial briefing by the MiFonda team, my team of fellow Brown students Anna Wang, Jaclyn Cohen, and Pranavi Lakshminarayanan began with our first drafts. One of our primary goals was maintaining a balance between visual creativity and an intuitive user experience, and we agreed horizontal video carousels were a positive media-forward implementation that wasn't visually overwhelming. We also decided to pursue a liking system, and thought it was a great way to engage users and promote interaction with the application, alongside communicate popular orders. Pulling from the various designs for further ideas of popups, symbols, sorting, and customization, we integrated our initial sketches and brainstorms into a Figma model to outline our plans to the client.


2. Feedback and Low-fi Wireframes

In a round of critique with our peers, the main area noted for improvement was consistent design across our mobile and desktop platforms. We wanted to ensure, moving forward, that the two versions were aligned for memorability and unification. Peers also encouraged us to think carefully about what elements were truly necessary, how the video carousel might operate with autoplay or pausing, and where we place certain features in relation to the purchase and checkout processes. When we sent our wireframes to the MiFonda team, they clarified their vision of focusing in on one restaurant rather than a selection, and compress the features into a single screen. Given this feedback, we revised our wireframes accordingly. We incorporated all of the feedback into our new designs; each page is specific to a store, there is more consideration of similar placement between the mobile and desktop versions, and the unique features were displayed prominently, among other smaller changes.


3. Hi-fi Prototyping

We built on the visual style guide provided and developed our hi-fi prototype. Fonda sent us a preliminary style guide containing the typography and color scheme. The founders gave us free reign over any style guide edits, so we developed various elements and symbols, including the drop-down menus, buttons, and banners. We also wanted to ensure accessibility in the usage of keyboard-navigable features and high-contrast colors. Using these guidelines, we developed our high-fidelity prototypes.


4. Founder feedback

The founders expressed a lot of positive feedback! They praised our design as “super innovative” and appreciated the visual-forward implementation, especially the videos. We discussed a few scenarios that might stretch the limitations of our design, such as multiple promo codes, or the potential of longer videos such as hearing from chefs. Overall, it was valuable to work with and hear from founders in a startup environment, and we are all extremely appreciative of the opportunity to practice our design and development skills in a more professional setting.


5. Final Thoughts

This project taught me a lot about effectively working in a team- both with other designers and with a real startup to meet the needs of everyone involved. Each designer pitched some great ideas that came together to create the final product, contributing to a synchronous design in both aesthetics and functionality. As an individual, I contributed most to the low-fidelity prototype development, alongside leading a lot of the communication with the MiFonda team.
